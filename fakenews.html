Fake News

Background

The development of internet become better and better, people can recieve lots of information amd post anything on the internet or social media. Some people start to spread the wrong message in order to achieve their goals such as producing fake news and misunderstand to attack the opposite political party or competitors.

Definition

  • According to the Cambridge Dictionary, fake news is false stories that appear to be news, spread on the internet or using other media, usually created to influence political views or as a joke.
  • Scholarly research has defined fake news as a form of falsehood intended to primarily deceive people by mimicking the look and feel of real news.

How does fake news spread?

Example

Fake News adout the pandemic: COVID-19 vaccine may cause infertility in women

According the suvery of Kaiser Family Foundation, it found out around 80% people believed ones of the fake news. Many people believed that message, however, that is not turth. Fertility and Sterility Reports point out that there was no difference in pregnancy rates after embryo transfer between women who developed antibodies to the virus due to vaccination or infection and those who did not. Besides, the data of Nature Reviews Immunology illustrate in clinical trials of vaccines, the odds of pregnancy were very similar between the vaccinated group and the unvaccinated control group.
